# Down with the King

Perhaps it was the Cruel Taxes, or the failed harvest. Perhaps it was poison in the wine, or daggers in the night, or maybe it was just the obnoxious sound of your voice that did it for them. Either way, someone wants you dead.

There are multiple ways to die in Rulers — either the Ruler can be killed, or a whole Realm ceases to exist.

## Ruler Death

The most common way to die in Rulers is for your Ruler to be assassinated, die in battle, or simply fall prey to natural causes. In the event of a Ruler dying, their Heir immediately takes the throne, and the Player assumes control of this Heir. However, this changeover of power may be seized as an opportunity for other Nobles to claim the throne, or question the Heir's legitimacy.

If the Ruler has not designated an Heir, then the Player is faced with a question: which significant NPC would they like to take control of? This NPC must reside in their own Realm, and immediately has access to all of that NPC's resources. They can act as a Ruler might (constructing Buildings, Recruiting Troops, and performing Political Actions) although they do not have the same authority as the Ruler does.

When playing as this surrogate, it is important for the player to act appropriately to that character. This means that the player can only act on knowledge this character would have, and not on what they learned as Ruler. The new Ruler of the Realm is to be decided by the N.O., and depending on the form of Governance, it may be possible for the Player to claim the throne.

## Realm Death

If every Settlement in a Realm is captured by a foreign power, or every member of its population killed or dispersed to the corners of the world, then the Realm has "died". In this event, the Ruler no longer has a Realm to govern.

If they have personally survived, the Player can continue roleplaying as that individual but they have lost all Settlements, Buildings and titles they may have had. The most loyal of Nobles and their Troops may choose to remain by the Ruler's side, but most will not. The Ruler may grab 10% of the Treasury before they are forced to flee.

The Ruler can turn to allies for help, and can be granted governance over a Settlement in another Realm, like any other Noble. While they may try to build a new Realm, or retake their old one, they are at the mercy of other Rulers to grant them titles, Settlements and Resources. They may only Recruit Troops and collect Taxes if the Ruler who granted them a Settlement to govern has given them permission to do so.
