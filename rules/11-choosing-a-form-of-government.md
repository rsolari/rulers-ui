# Choosing a Form of Government

Some Rulers are ruled by a family through a hereditary title, others may be ruled by a Religious leader, or by an elected official. However your Realm is run, it is an important element to define!

Choose one of the forms of governance from the list below. If a player wants to create a new one, they may negotiate with the N.O., but the important factor is that the players' rule is absolute. In-game actions will be very difficult for the player to justify if everything they decide has to be approved by someone else.[^1]

## Forms of Government

**Monarch** — You were born to rule, and you will rule for your whole life, at which point your heir will take over. This position is for life, and is automatically passed onto your heir.

**Elected Monarch** — You still rule for life like a monarch does, but you were elected by the Nobility of your Realm. After your death, they will elect a new monarch from one of the noble families. You cannot be voted out of office. This position is for life.

**Council** — You are the chairman of a council who governs the Realm. While others on the council will have a say in state politics, you ultimately have the final say. This position is for life, but the council can call a vote to elect a new chairman.

**Ecclesiastical** — You are both the head of the church, and the head of the Realm. Create an appropriate Society or Order for your Ruler to also be the head of.

**Consortium** — The Realm is governed by a trading company, and you sit at the head of its council. This position operates as Council, but with more bribery.[^2] Create a Guild for your Ruler to also be the Chairperson of.

**Magistrate** — You were appointed by a higher authority to be in charge of your Realm. You can be replaced at any time by the higher authority. This authority has to be defined at creation, and must be an NPC which the N.O. controls.[^3]

**Warlord** — You are the commander of the military, and the head of state. This position can operate as Elected Monarch or Council, depending on the strength of your Armies.

[^1]: None of that 'Democracy' nonsense here, sunshine!
[^2]: Sorry, I meant "gifts".
[^3]: This includes celestial beings, spirits or gods.
