# Nobles

Nobles form the upper echelon of your Realm. They are the elite, the land owners, the scholars and its commanders. They govern your towns, run the Church and lead your armies. The Noble elite in your Realm do not necessarily have to have been born Nobles. The elite in your Realm may stem from its most successful merchant families, or from its most influential Abbeys.

Wherever the elite come from, each Noble must be a member of a family, clan or house. Call it what you will, but they have allegiances that come before their loyalty to the Ruler. Each Noble has their own motivations and goals. They will want to increase their holdings and therefore their wealth and power.

## Titles

Titles can be awarded to any Noble NPC as a normal part of the day to day running of your Realm, like Recruitment or Taxes.[^22] Titles include: Lord of a Settlement, Head of a Guild, Order or Society, General of an Army or Sheriff of a Garrison.

Nobles are needed to govern any settlement that is not your capital, and to lead an Army outside of your Realm. Before the start of the game, choose a Noble who is a fitting person to lead any Guilds, Orders or Societies your Realm has. Once the game starts, those Organisations will choose their own leaders. Make sure all these positions are filled before the start of the game.

Awarding a Title to a Noble may please or irk them, and new Titles can be created to achieve this effect.[^23]

## Estates

Every Noble who governs a Settlement requires an Estate. This represents the Stipend they live on, as well as the size of their home, number of servants, and so on.

| Estate | Influence | Cost p/Season |
|---|---|---|
| Meagre | Village | 125g |
| Comfortable | Town | 250g |
| Ample | City | 500g |
| Substantial | Territory | 1,000g |
| Luxurious | Ruler | 2,000g |

As the size, or significance of the Settlement increases, the Noble in question will demand a larger and larger Estate. They may also demand a larger estate for excellence in their service to the Ruler and the Realm, through Battle, Diplomacy or possibly even Subterfuge.[^24]

While a Noble will always want more, they will accept an Estate suitable for their station. For each Noble who governs a Settlement, the Ruler must pay them the following Upkeep for their Estate. A Ruler does not need to pay for their own upkeep.

## Commanding Troops

A Noble who governs a Settlement is also in charge of every Troop assigned to that Settlement. These Troops owe allegiance to their governing Noble first, and then their Ruler. A Ruler will therefore want to ensure that all of their Nobles are loyal enough, or simply fear betraying them. A Ruler should also make sure they are acting in the best interests of the Realm, as their subjects will prioritise the Realm, or themselves, over the Ruler if they feel it is necessary.

If a collection of Troops leaves the Realm to go on a military expedition, then this group is an Army and must be led by a Noble. This can be the Ruler themselves. Once given their orders by their Ruler, it is then up to the General to interpret their orders as best they can. "Attack the City of Aggraia" leaves a lot of room for the General to decide how much of the City should be left when they're done. Neither does it necessarily specify if the City should be Captured.

As Orders are messages that take time to travel from one location to another, giving an Army new orders can be difficult. Therefore, it is important that their orders are clear from the beginning as a Noble will interpret them as they see fit.
