# Armies

## Recruiting Troops

Whether a Ruler wishes to see their banner flying from every tower in the land, or simply to protect the livelihood of their people, a Realm needs Troops. As every subject of the Realm owes allegiance to their Lord, and the Lords in turn owe service to their Ruler, calling Troops to action is a relatively simple affair.[^32]

However, someone has to toil the land and make the wares people use in their daily lives. Depending on the size of the Realm's Settlements and their number, will determine how many Troops a Realm can support.

| Settlement | Max Troops |
|---|---|
| Village | 6 Troops each |
| Town | 10 Troops each |
| City | 20 Troops each |

It also takes time to mobilise Troops and inspect their equipment. Each Settlement also determines how many Troops may be Recruited each Season:

| Settlement | Recruitment per Season |
|---|---|
| Village | 4 Troops |
| Town | 6 Troops |
| City | 8 Troops |

**Basic Troops** might be fortunate enough to receive some training, but even with their simple equipment, they can prove very effective in large numbers.

**Elite Troops** come from the Nobility of your Realm. They have much more time to not just practise, but to even master their martial training. In addition, they can afford to procure much more expensive equipment.

Once recruited, Troops immediately report to the Garrison they were assigned. This can be a Settlement or a Fortification. They are commanded by the governing Noble of this Settlement, or must be assigned to the command of a Noble.

If a collection of Troops — an Army — wants to travel to a location that does not have a Garrison, they must be led by a Noble. Nobles who fight well in your service and lead you to Victory will expect an increase in their holdings and rank.

It costs nothing to Recruit Troops, but it does cost to keep them fed, train them once recruited, and maintain their equipment. This is all done by paying Upkeep. In order to recruit a type of Troop, the Realm needs access to the necessary equipment. These armaments[^32] usually come from Buildings within the Realm. However, a Realm is treated as having the same equipment of Realms it is trading with for the purposes of Recruiting Troops. Although, Troops recruited in this way cost **25% more**, as the equipment has to be purchased from the other Realm.

## Troop List: Basic Units

### Spearmen
**Equipment:** Spear | **Bonus:** +1 vs. Cavalry | **Armour:** Light | **Requires:** — | **Cost:** 250g

> *"Give a man a sharp stick, maybe with a metal point, and he can do enough damage to a man when he's frightened."* — A Mord orientation day favourite

Spearmen are simple but effective Troops. They can even be formidable in numbers, when braced against charging Cavalry.

### Archers
**Equipment:** Bow | **Bonus:** +2 vs. Light | **Armour:** Light | **Requires:** — | **Cost:** 250g

Archers are exceptional ranged Troops, out-ranging both Crossbows and Muskets. Their accuracy is unparalleled. However, it requires a lot of training to reach such a level of skill.

### Shieldbearers
**Equipment:** Spear, Shield | **Bonus:** +1 vs. Cavalry, +1 vs. Light | **Armour:** Light | **Requires:** Armourer | **Cost:** 500g

These Armoured troops carry shields for added defence. While they are not of the quality Nobles can afford, it is enough to keep these hardy Troops on the frontline.

### Berserkers
**Equipment:** Two-handed Weapon | **Bonus:** +2 vs. Light | **Armour:** Light | **Requires:** Weaponsmith | **Cost:** 500g

> *"Give a frightened man a Spear and he can fight. Give a frightening man a Great-Axe and be prepared to soil your Armour."* — Old Mord Saying

Bare-chested and screaming a harrowing battlecry, Berserkers are a terror on the battlefield. They will tear through lightly armoured troops, but have little to no defence themselves.

### Crossbowmen
**Equipment:** Crossbow | **Bonus:** +1 vs. Armoured | **Armour:** Light | **Requires:** Bowyer | **Cost:** 250g

Crossbows are more complicated to make than bows, but are much easier to learn to use. Their bolts pack enough of a punch to pierce through armour, but they lack the range and accuracy of Archers.

### Harquebusiers ("Harlequins")
**Equipment:** Harquebus gun | **Bonus:** +2 vs. Armoured | **Armour:** Light | **Requires:** Gunsmith | **Cost:** 250g

Nicknamed "Harlequins" for the wailing sound of their shot tearing through flesh, these Troops require little to no training at all. While brutally effective against both light and well-armoured foes, they have exceedingly poor range and accuracy, and take a very long time to reload. Their strategy, then, is to fill fifty feet in front of them with enough flying shrapnel that these hindrances no longer matter.

### Light Cavalry
**Equipment:** Lance, Mount, Short-Sword | **Bonus:** +2 vs. Light | **Armour:** Light | **Requires:** Stables | **Cost:** 750g

On mounted steeds, these riders charge into battle. Although not as heavily armoured as their Noble counterparts, they excel at scouting and disrupting poorly defended Troops.

## Troop List: Elite Units

### Pikemen
**Equipment:** Halberd / Polearm | **Bonus:** +2 vs. Cavalry | **Armour:** Armoured | **Requires:** Armourer | **Cost:** 750g

Equipped with heavier armour and a more menacing weapon than Spearmen, coupled with the fact they may actually have had some training, Pikemen make for formidable defence against cavalry.

### Swordsmen
**Equipment:** Sword, Shield | **Bonus:** +2 vs. Light | **Armour:** Armoured | **Requires:** Armourer, Weaponsmith | **Cost:** 750g

A shield to catch arrows, armour to deflect blows, and a fine blade to cleave flesh — these are the tools of the Swordsmen. Steadily marching forward, they can devastate lightly armoured opponents.

### Fusiliers
**Equipment:** Harquebus gun | **Bonus:** +1 vs. Light, +1 vs. Armoured | **Armour:** Light | **Requires:** Gunsmith | **Cost:** 500g

Between rank and file "Harlequins" and their more accurate counterparts, there is little difference except pomposity. The only difference of note is that the elite can afford hand-rifled guns, allowing them greater accuracy and longer range.

### Cavalry
**Equipment:** Lance, Shield, Sword, Mount | **Bonus:** +4 vs. Light | **Armour:** Armoured, Mounted | **Requires:** Armourer, Weaponsmith, Stables | **Cost:** 1,000g

With thundering hooves and shining armour, a Cavalry charge terrifies all unfortunate enough to stand in their way. These Armoured and Mounted Troops are equipped with lances and shields for the charge, as well as a shortsword for close quarters fighting.

### Mounted Archers
**Equipment:** Bow, Mount | **Bonus:** +2 vs. Light | **Armour:** Light, Mounted | **Requires:** Stables | **Cost:** 750g

> *"Horses are not just good for getting you close to an enemy, they are also excellent for getting you very, very far away."* — Shadowfox Military Guidance Document Vol. 47

These immensely skilled archers are also capable of riding a mount while firing their bow. In order to achieve this feat though, they must be lightly armoured. As such, they are susceptible to melee fighting.

### Dragoons
**Equipment:** Harquebus gun, Mount | **Bonus:** +1 vs. Light, +1 vs. Armoured | **Armour:** Light, Mounted | **Requires:** Gunsmith, Stables | **Cost:** 750g

> *"The rumbling roar of a mounted charge, mixed with the wail of shrapnel and the cries of men, is truly the most terrible sound one could hear in battle."* — Unknown Cyrian diarist

Equipped with a Harquebus musket, gunpowder and shot, these marksmen fire into the melee from afar. While they are not capable of firing and riding with the same accuracy as Mounted Archers, their mounts provide them with the same mobility on the battlefield.
