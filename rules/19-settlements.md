# Settlements

A Realm is made up of Settlements where people live and work. Wherever there is work, there are people, and people need homes.[^27] Settlements naturally occur in these locations, such as where Resources are collected, or where Armies and Nobles gather, as these can also be a lucrative place for businesses.

Settlements come in three sizes: **Villages**, **Towns** and **Cities**. Each Settlement is governed by a Noble in the Realm. They are seats of power where Wealth is generated, and where Food is brought to market. Most Settlements are located near a Resource site, and the Industries this Resource facilitates are the primary source of income for the Settlement.

## Building Slots

Settlements are not all the same. Some are more Rural than others and therefore have more agrarian Industries, while particularly Urban Settlements will rely on others to provide them with their Food. Each size of Settlement has a certain number of Building slots:

- Villages have **4** Building Slots
- Towns have **6** Building Slots
- Cities have **8** Building Slots

When it comes to the sizes of Buildings a Settlement can have, there are some limitations:

- A Village cannot have a building bigger than Medium
- A Town cannot have a building bigger than Large
- A City has no limit

## Settlement Growth

When a Settlement is 1 Building slot away from having all its slots full, then it grows in size to the next level of Settlement. A City is the largest Settlement size in Rulers, and is the only one that can be completely Urban (i.e. fill all of its Building slots).

For example: Profiteria, the principal Town of the Shadowfox Trading Company, has 6 Building slots. 4 of these slots are taken up by 4 Buildings, mostly belonging to the major Guilds. This means that Profiteria only has room for two more Buildings before it will expand into a City.

If a Village has a Fort constructed, or a Town gains a Castle, these are also times when the Settlement size would grow — but only if the Players and N.O. agree that they are sufficiently active to warrant growth.[^28]

## Governing Nobles

Every Settlement must have a Noble governing it. For Villages and Towns a simple Knight will suffice, but Cities will require their own Lords. The larger and the more important the Settlement, the more the Noble governing it will expect to be paid according to their status.

## New Settlements

A Ruler cannot order a Settlement to be built. They can only grow naturally from situations that demand them. Castles and Forts may grow into Villages if the Players and N.O. agree that they are sufficiently active to support a community of families.

If these conditions are met, or there are others which appease the N.O., then a new Settlement can be founded. Every new Settlement starts out as a Village. It is also possible for a Settlement to exist without being attached to a Resource site (such as those which grow from Forts and Castles). Every Player Realm gets one such Settlement at the start of the game, that they may place as they wish.

## Defences

A Settlement which has begun life as a Town will automatically have Wooden Walls and a Gatehouse. A Settlement which began life as a City automatically has Stone Walls and a Stone Gatehouse. If a Settlement grows in size to the next level of Settlement, it does not gain these defences. The same is true of a Fort or Castle that grows into a Settlement — however, they do keep whatever defences they had.

## Population

Populations for individual Settlements are not tracked numerically. That is for the narrative of your game to allude to. However, population is relevant to Armies and migration.

The scale of populations in your game will depend on the world the N.O. and the Players have created. What makes sense for the Races and Civilisations in your world? By default, we assume that 1 Troop of military units is 1,000 persons. However, this could just as easily represent 100 or 10,000 warriors.

For example, 1 Troop of Onor'Tok Berserkers represents 100 Warriors. This is because they are tough and immensely strong, and according to our story, are supposed to be able to take on 1,000 soldiers from any other race. However, it would be tough to justify this if 1 Troop from another Realm represents 10,000 soldiers!

It is up to the Players and the N.O. to discuss at the start of the game what they have in mind for the scale of populations. The important thing to consider is consistency, as this will allow your game to make narrative sense to everyone playing.
