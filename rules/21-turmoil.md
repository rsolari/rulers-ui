# Turmoil

Nobody likes having Taxes levied upon them, especially those who are struggling to feed their families and scratch out a living. And these same people can be pretty terrifying when armed with pitchforks and torches!

Turmoil represents unrest in your Realm. It can come from Taxation, Food Shortages, War, Disasters or just from the natural obnoxiousness of the Ruler. As Turmoil rises in your Realm, the greater the chances are of the people rioting or rebelling. Nobles who aren't fond of your rule may seize such an opportunity to overthrow the Ruler.

## Calculating Turmoil

Turmoil can come from a number of different sources, but all ultimately fuel disorder and tension in the Realm across all social ranks. While Turmoil can raise to unknown heights of discontent, it is not possible for Turmoil to become a negative number or in any way create a positive sentiment in your Realm.

### Taxes

The biggest cause of Turmoil is Taxation, with higher tax rates increasing Turmoil further. This Turmoil persists as long as the Tax Rate is in place.

| Tax Rate | Turmoil |
|---|---|
| Low | 4 |
| Fair | 6 |
| Cruel | 10 |

### Nobles

Another source of Turmoil is displeased Nobles. While their indignation does not necessarily reflect the whole populace, they are the ones who will take advantage of Turmoil turning into action. Turmoil added in this manner lasts for 1 Year (4 Seasons).

| Issue | Turmoil |
|---|---|
| Another Noble being appointed a Title they wanted | 1 |
| Not paid a fitting Estate | 2 |
| Their Settlement being lost to another Realm | 5 |

### Guilds, Orders & Societies

The Turmoil from Guilds, Orders and Societies represents their influential sway over public sentiment. Turmoil added in this manner lasts for 1 Year (4 Seasons).

### Events in the Realm

Below are guidelines for other events and actions that would also increase Turmoil. The N.O. is free to award Turmoil to Realms they think are deserving of it.

| Incident | Turmoil |
|---|---|
| New Trade Route opened | −1 / +1 |
| War declared | +2 |
| Peace declared | −2 |
| A Festival is held | −2 |
| Food Shortages | +4 |
| A Settlement is Attacked | +4 |
| A Settlement is captured | +6 |

## Turmoil Turns into Action

Winter is the most trying Season for the people of your Realm. When this Season comes around, your leadership will be tested.

For every point of Turmoil you have, rounding up, roll 1d6. A roll of 1–2 is considered a Failure. For every Failure rolled, resentment boils over into action. Refer to the table below to see what acts your people have been driven to in their anger and desperation.

The details of the population's frustration are for the N.O. to decide. People who have suffered a bad harvest will have different objectives to those who might blame the Ruler's warmongering for heavy taxes. If the peasantry is angry, which nobles would side with their cause? Would they do it out of sympathy, or simply to further their political goals? It is important for the N.O. to consider the individuals behind the Turmoil, what their motivations are, and what their ultimate objective is.

| Failures | Result |
|---|---|
| 1 | Written demands for change |
| 2 | A Noble supports these demands |
| 3 | Protests (1d3 locations) |
| 4 | Strikes (1d3 locations) |
| 5 | Riots (1d3 locations) |
| 6 | Armed Revolt (1d3 locations) |
| 7 | Armed revolt, supported by Nobles |
| 8 | A Rebellion led by Nobles |
| 9+ | Rioting, Looting, Arson, Armed Revolt and Rebellion (1d6 locations) |
