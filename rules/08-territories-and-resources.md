# Territories & Resources

We now have a world! However, we don't know where our borders are going to be. Every player is going to need their own Realm, and so will all the NPCs. We will also need a couple of neutral areas to allow for expansion.

For every 2 players, have one NPC Realm and one neutral Territory, rounding up. Every Player will also need one Territory for their own Realm. For example, a game with 5 players would have 3 NPC Rulers, 3 Neutral Territories, and 5 Player Territories, for a total of 11 Territories.

Neutral Territories are likely to be uninhabited, or they may house [Settlements](19-settlements.md) with no centralised government, such as Nomadic tribes or an Amish Orc Commune.[^8]

Once you know how many Territories you are likely to need, split the world up as necessary. Use mountains, rivers and coastlines to form natural borders. It doesn't matter if Territories are not all the same size, as this will not affect the time it takes to cross them or the amount of resources they have.

## Placing Realms

Most players will come to the game with a clear idea of what they want to do with their civilisation. Don't let them! A little bit of randomness can make an okay concept into a great character.

The greatest success story of this was in our first play-test. Cyria was a civilisation of mechanical slaves who had escaped an oppressive Empire and were led by their prophet, and now ecclesiastical leader,[^9] across the desert to their new home. It was the fact that the player had been randomly placed beside a desert that gave their leader this biblical pilgrimage, its Arabic themes, and even the kingdom's name. This gave it a tremendous amount of depth that the original concept was at first missing.

Number all Territories on the map and roll appropriately sized dice to determine where each player will be placed. This is done at the N.O.'s discretion, as it is in their interests to place NPCs and neutral territories strategically.

## Settlements in the World

At the start of your game of Rulers, every Resource site in the world, including every NPC and Player Realm, has a [Settlement](19-settlements.md).

If there are Resources in Neutral Territories not owned by any Realm, then create "Free Peoples" who live there, or use the rules for randomly generating Settlements.

## Generating Resources

For each Player or significant NPC region, roll on the Basic Resource Table 3 times, and the Luxury table once. These Resources are known to all players. For neutral regions, or ones with smaller city-states, roll on the Common and Luxury Resources twice. These Resources are not known to all players.

## Understanding Resources

It would be very difficult for a society to function at all, let alone rise to becoming a significant civilisation, if it was completely without metal Ore, Timber or any other Resource. Resource Sites instead represent an ability to support entire [Industries](24-resources-and-industries.md) to the extent that they have a significant impact on the Realm and can even export the product.

**No Ore:** If a Realm is without Iron Ore Resources, it is assumed that they at least have Bronze (an alloy of Copper and Tin). This does not put the Realm at any specific disadvantage. Bronze is poured into a mould and cast — they therefore have no problem making swords, arrowheads, pole-axes etc. However, more complex metal-working may be beyond them without imports of Iron, which can be heated and forged, allowing it to be bent and folded. The revolution of the Iron Age was not that it was superior to Bronze, but rather that it was easier to work more complex parts. If you're interested, take a look at what the Ancient Chinese Emperor Qin and his Terracotta Warriors were able to achieve with bronze, including advanced mechanisms and crossbows.

**No Timber:** What is a home made of if there is not an abundance of timber? The answer might be cultures like the Mongolians with their Urt tents, or something like the Ancient Britons. Wattle & Daub[^10] is a technique of hovel making where upright branches have longer branches woven in between them, until you have a circular home that looks like a willow basket. To keep out water and keep in the heat, a mixture of grit, fibres and horse manure is packed into the walls and coated with a plaster. By default, we assume homes are made in this manner. Again, not having Timber Resource simply means that it is not available in abundance, and cannot fulfil requirements for Rulers.

**No Clay:** Clay isn't that important, right? Wrong! History is the study of pottery. Okay, that's not entirely true, but it is 90% of what Archaeologists dig up. Bowls, plates, bottles, medicine flasks, chamber pots — if it held a fluid before the advent of plastic, it was probably made of pottery. So we can explain a Realm that doesn't have Clay as making a lot of these items out of wood, or bone, or maybe bronze. However, if the Realm also doesn't have wood, then bone is going to have a tall order to fill, as it is quite limited by the shape of bones.[^11] Get creative. Does your Realm have an abundance of Iron or Stone? Perhaps their master craftsmen use that instead.

**No Stone:** The simplest Resource for a Realm to not have.[^12] Stone is mostly only used for [Fortifications](26-buildings.md#fortifications), and as such a Realm can happily survive without it. Stone does offer benefits though, such as allowing Players to define their [Buildings](26-buildings.md) as being made of Stone, and therefore being fireproof.
