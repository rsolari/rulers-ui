# Siege Units

Siege Units Siege weapons operate differently to other Troops. While
other Troops are Recruited, Siege Units are instead Constructed. Each
Siege weapon takes a number of Sea - sons (turns) to be Constructed as
listed. Upkeep is paid during the Construction phase aswell. Dice rolls
are resolved normally with Siege Weapons and their bonuses are applied
and stacked in the same way. They are treated as Basic Troops when
rolling dice pools. In addition, when resolving Combat Rolls against
Fortifi - cations, Siege Weapons have additional bonus Damage as listed.
In Full Combat, a Siege Weapon cannot Move and Act. If a Siege Weapon is
Routed or Defeated, then it is the engineers manning the Siege Weapon
that are lost. The Siege Weapon survives and is captured by the enemy
forces. If a Siege Weapon itself is Wounded or Crushed, then it is

would roll out.Armour Types Armour comes in three types: Light, Armoured
or Mounted. Light refers to Lightly armoured Troops, which allows them
to be relatively fast and manoeuvrable. Examples would include Leather
and Hide armour. Troops with this armour are particularly weak to
Crush - ing and Piercing weapons. In combat they are faster over all
terrains. If an army entirely consists of Light armoured units, then
they may move across the world map with +1 speed. Armoured describes
much heavier defensive garments. These are effective, but restrict
movement. Examples would include chain mail and plate armour. Troops
with this armour are weak to Piercing weapons. In combat they are slower
than Lightly armoured troops and struggle with difficult terrain.
Mounted describes Troops riding Cavalry into battle. Mounted troops can
be Light or Armoured. Mounted Troops are particularly weak to Reach
weap - ons, but they have an Advantage of +1 dice against Light - ly
armoured Troops. In combat they are the fastest over any terrain. If an
army entirely consists of Mounted troops, then that army may move across
the world map with +2 speed if they wear Light armour, or +1 one if they
are Armoured. Shields also affect the defensive capabilities of Troops.
Any Troop equipped with a Shield, ignores a Combat Roll difference of 1,
during Full Combat. For instance, if a Troop of Shieldbearers are fired
upon by Archers, the Archers rolling 5 successes and the Shield bearers
4 successes, this is still treated as an equal roll, as the arrows
glance of their shields.
