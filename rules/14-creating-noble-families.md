# Creating Noble Families

Roll 1d4 — this is how many Noble Families there are in your Realm, including the [Ruler's](12-creating-your-ruler.md) own family. For every family, roll 1d6. This determines how many leading members the family has. One of them must be the Head of the family, while the others may be Aunts, Uncles, Siblings or Grandparents.

For each leading family member, roll 1d10: on a 1–5 they are male, on a 6–10 they are female.

To determine the age of each of them, roll 1d10 on the table below:

| Roll (1d10) | Age |
|---|---|
| 1 | Infant |
| 2–3 | Adolescent |
| 4–8 | Adult |
| 9–10 | Elderly |

For each family member who is Adult or older, roll 1d4 to determine how many Children they have. Roll as usual to determine their age, but discount all Elderly rolls.

Fill in the gaps as you see fit. These results are to give you a starting point. For instance, spouses are not rolled for, but they are certainly members of the family. You can use rolls you've made to account for them, or add them in as you want to. Perhaps some died in childbirth, perhaps some left to start their own families. Which families did these Spouses come from, or are they from another Realm? We encourage Players to have their Noble families linked with other Players, or NPCs, wherever the genealogy allows it.
