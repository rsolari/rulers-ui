# Welcome to Rulers

Welcome to Rulers Rulers is a tabletop roleplaying game of conquest and
politics, where players will craft their own civilisation, and forge
their own dynasty. The game is played with 4-6 Players and 1 Narrative
Overlord, whose job it is to "referee" the game. To take actions in the
game, Players will send Reports to the Narrative Overlord by email,
letter, private courier or carrier pigeon (as you prefer). This book the
Rulers: Core Rulebook - details the Rulers System as well as the
instructions on how to play the Rulers roleplaying game. Tabletop
Roleplaying games are wonderful social activity of group storytelling,
and Rulers is no different. As such, the game all happens in your
collective imagination. Feel free to use maps, diagrams and even
figurines if you want to, but do not feel obliged to do so. We'll get
into all the detailed rules of Building up an Empire, Recruiting Armies,
and Converting the masses. Butfirst, let's look at the some of the basic
concepts The Narrative Overlord A game of Rulers has 3-6 Players and one
Narrative Over - lord (N.O.). It is the task of the N.O. to run the game
and fundamentally captain the game as a sort of referee for the story.
It is up to the Narrative Overlord to make sure all Player actions
remain reasonable, that there are consequences for their actions, and to
aid the narrative development of the game. While at the same time, an
N.O. should make sure that everyone is having fun. From time to time,
this may mean saying N.O. to their players!1 The N.O also controls all
Rulers, factions and organisa - tions not directly controlled by the
Players themselves. This includes all Nobles, Guilds, Orders and
Societies in a Player's Realm, who each have their own political agen
da. For this reason, you can have a second N.O. if help is needed to
control all these political entities. Alternatively, hand over control
of certain NPCs to Players. Just make sure that they act according to
that character, and not the interests of the Player. The N.O. goes first
in a game of Rulers . Political Actions are not limited for entities the
N.O. controls, but they must still be able to pay for any Buildings,
Troops or oth - er actions they might perform. 1 I had no intention of
keeping this pun, but my playtesters thought it was hilarious. Y ou are
now stuck with it.The Players Players in a game of Rulers, control the
Ruler of a state. This is their in game avatar. Maybe your family line
has always ruled, perhaps you were elected to your current position, or
you were quite simply ordained to rule by \[Insert Deity/Pantheon of
choice here\]. Whatever the reason, you Rule! Each Player controls the
Ruler of their Realm, and can roleplay actions as the Rulers they
control. As Ruler you govern your Realm, the Territory you con - trol.
You can levy Taxes to raise funds which can be used to Construct
Buildings and Recruit Troops. In addition to this, a Ruler can perform
Political Actions. Your responsibilities are to protect your Realm and
the property of your people. It is not your duty to meddle in their
everyday lives1. The economy of your Realm will largely take care of
itself and does not need a Ruler's micro-management. The people of your
Realm, from Farmer to Nobleman, will be unhappy if the Realm runs out of
Food, or if their livelihood is threatened, but these responsibilities
are not enshrined in law. They will simply take matters into their own
hands if there is too much Turmoil . The Objective The objective of a
game of Rulers can vary. It can be discussed by all the Players and the
N.O., but the N.O. has the final say. Ultimately, it is the N.O.'s job
to decide where the story goes. A game of Rulers can played be until an
invading army has been defeated, the secrets of an ancient civilisation
discovered, or as long as you are having fun playing. Whatever the goal
may be, it is a good idea for a Ruler to have their own goal in mind,
and to roleplay their Ruler's individual ambitions. 1 Although not every
