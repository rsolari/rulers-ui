# Trade

Monopolies and Prices After a trade link has been opened, a Realm will
import all products it does not have. If a Realm is open to multi - ple
trade links, then it will always buy the best quality at the lowest
price . A Realm cannot import the same product from multiple sources.
Products are always traded in monopolies. For example, the Shadowfox
Trading Company is im - porting lacquerware from Mordravale along their
Trade Route, but now it has begun Trading with the mysterious Crimson
Empire, who offer lacquerware inlaid with gold. This new product is of a
superior quality, so naturally the S.T.C buys their lacquerware from the
Crimson Empire instead, taking this export away from Mordravale. Quality
is determined by the number of ingredients a product has and if it has
the High Quality bonus or not. The High Quality always trumps Products
with the same number of ingredients. Quality Table Lowest Quality Basic

Quality HQ + 3 IngredientsTrade Trade occurs when two Realms, NPC or PC,
sign a Trade Agreement. All goods which both Realms do not currently
have are automatically imported. A negligible amount of other goods,
books, religions and ideas, are also traded as part of the storytelling
of the game. This Trade Route connects two Realms through a specif - ic
Settlement in each Realm. This increases the Wealth generated by that
Settlement, by 5% of its total, for every Product being exported. For
Example, the Shadowfox Trading Company can offer Porcelain, Jewels and
Gold along its Trade Route with Mordravale, as it does not produce any
of these fine products itself. This increases the wealth generated by
the town of Profiteria, by 15%! Meanwhile Brasaholm gains just 5% to its
wealth for only having lacquerware to offer Shadowfox. However, as the
only producers of such a fine product, they are unlikely to lose this
trade any time soon. In addition to this immediate financial gain, a
Realm is treated as having any Resource its trade partner has, for the
purposes of Constructing Buildings, Recruiting Troops, or performing
Special Actions. So although a Realm may not quarry any Stone itself, if
it is trading with a Realm that does, it is treated as having these
materials itself, as it now importing them from the Realm it trades
with. For example, Mordravale lacked the Stone it needed to build its
new Fortifications. By opening a Trade Route with Cyria, it can now
build a new Castle (albeit at an added cost) buy importing the Stone it
needed. At the same time, Cyria can begin Recruiting Crossbow Troops
(also at an added cost), as they can now hire ex - pert instructors from
Mordravale, and import fine Mord Crossbow for their new defensive
regiment. However, keen to keep its Gunsmithing technology to itself,
Amethysia is keen to keep its borders shut to mer - chants, but that has
made the issue of Food Shortages a tense issue in the capital city.
