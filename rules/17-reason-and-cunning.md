# Reason and Cunning

Of course, a General may disregard their orders entirely. It is entirely down to their personality, and the story of your Realm.

## Agents of the Crown

[Nobles](16-nobles.md) can be given orders to act as Agents for their [Ruler](12-creating-your-ruler.md). This may involve them travelling to another Realm as a Diplomat, a General, or even a Spy. The advantage of this is that a Noble Agent can act independently, and so a Ruler doesn't need to send a constant stream of [Messages](06-actions-in-rulers.md#sending-messages) to command them.

To do this, a Ruler can attach one of their [Political Actions](06-actions-in-rulers.md#political-actions) to a Noble instead. This still counts as using one of their political actions. However, a Noble Agent will act as they see fit, and follow the orders they have been given, to their best interests.[^25] It is up to the N.O. to decide how successful, if at all, the Agent is in following their orders. They may use Reason and Cunning rolls to help determine this.

## Reason and Cunning

The N.O. may deem it necessary to use the Skills a Noble has in Reason and Cunning to determine the outcome of events. Perhaps they have been sent to Negotiate a Peace Treaty, Arrange a [Trade](25-trade.md) route, or maybe it is their ability to lead their [Troops](27-armies.md) in [battle](29-warfare.md) which must be tested.

The use of these skills is to help the N.O. and the Players resolve uncertain situations. It is not necessary for players to jump through dice rolls to convince an NPC to start trading with them. It is however valuable for maintaining fairness. As always, it is up to the N.O. to discuss with the players how best to advance the narrative of the game, as is reasonable and fun to them.

There are two Skill types: **Reason** and **Cunning**.

Reason is an individual's ability to apply knowledge to a problem, while Cunning is something which cannot be learnt from books, but only through practice.

When a Noble's Skill is needed, roll 5d6. This roll is opposed by the other Noble's dice pool or a Difficulty determined by the N.O. A roll of 5–6 is a success. If the 'Attacking' Noble has more successes, their action was successful. If the 'Defending' Noble has more successes, then the 'Attacking' Noble's attempt has failed.

It is up to the N.O. and the player to decide which skill is most applicable for what the Noble is attempting to do.

### Reason

Reason represents a Character's ability to apply learned knowledge and logic to a situation. Reason is used in any situation where a convincing argument has to be made through evidence and analysis. Examples might be: Converting people to your Faith, Requesting a [Trade Route](25-trade.md), [Besieging](30-besieging.md) a [Castle](26-buildings.md#castle), or using knowledge of swordsmanship to strike a perfectly timed blow.

### Cunning

Cunning represents a Noble's ability to act on intuition, and feel a situation. Cunning is used in any situation where lying or subterfuge is required. Examples could be: a Noble trying to hide their intentions, deceiving someone with false information, spying on an individual, Sabotaging defences, or using unorthodox tricks in combat.

[^25]: Tip: Don't send the bitter war-scarred General whose wife was killed by Mordravale Inquisitors to do the peace negotiations. It won't end well.
