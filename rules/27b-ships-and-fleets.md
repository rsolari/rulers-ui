# Ships & Fleets

Where Armies march on land, Fleets command the waters — rivers, coastlines, and the open ocean. A Fleet can blockade a port, escort [Trade Routes](25-trade.md) across the sea, or land Troops behind enemy lines. Like [Armies](27-armies.md), a Fleet must be commanded by a [Noble](16-nobles.md), who in this role is referred to as its Admiral.

## Constructing Ships

Ships are Constructed, not Recruited. Unlike [Troops](27-armies.md), they require time, resources, and often specialised buildings before they can set sail. The Construction time is listed in Seasons. Upkeep must be paid during the Construction phase, just as with [Siege Weapons](28-siege-units.md).

Once constructed, a Ship must be assigned to a [Port](26-buildings.md#port). It cannot sail without one. A Port can station any number of Ships, as the N.O. deems reasonable for its size.

## Fleets

A collection of Ships forms a Fleet. Like an Army, a Fleet requires a Noble to command it — this Noble is called the Admiral. An Admiral leads the Fleet in naval operations, including combat, blockades, and the transport of Troops.

A Fleet without a Noble in command cannot leave port. Ships not assigned to a Fleet may remain in a Port but cannot be given orders.

## Light and Heavy Ships

Ships come in two types: **Light** and **Heavy**.

**Light Ships** are fast and manoeuvrable. They can navigate rivers as well as coastal and ocean waters, depending on the vessel. In naval combat, Light Ships can be outgunned by Heavy Ships, but their speed and reach give them other advantages.

**Heavy Ships** are imposing and powerful. Their weight and draught restricts them to deeper coastal and ocean waters — they cannot navigate rivers. In naval combat they carry more firepower, but they are slower to respond to changing winds and currents.

## Navigation

Where a Ship can sail depends on its type and rating:

- **Rivers** — Only certain Light Ships can navigate rivers. A Settlement must have a [Port](26-buildings.md#port) to embark or disembark Ships and Troops on a river.
- **Coastal Waters** — Most Ships can sail along coastlines. A Port is required to station Ships or transfer Troops.
- **Open Ocean** — Only ocean-going Ships may cross open ocean. Attempting to sail open ocean in a Ship not rated for it risks catastrophic loss at the N.O.'s discretion.

## Weather

Before a Fleet can act in a Season, the N.O. rolls 1d6 to determine the weather. Each Ship has a minimum weather threshold — if the roll falls below that threshold, Ships of that type cannot sail and must remain in port. A roll of **1** represents storms so severe that all Ships must make for harbour; those caught at sea suffer consequences at the N.O.'s discretion.

| Weather Roll | Conditions |
|---|---|
| 1 | Severe storms — all Ships must return to port or risk loss |
| 2–4 | Rough seas — only ocean-going Ships (threshold 2+) may sail |
| 5–6 | Calm — all Ships may sail |

## Naval Combat

Naval combat is resolved in the same way as land combat (see [Warfare](29-warfare.md)), using the same dice pools, Quick Combat and Full Combat rules. Ships use the same Advantage and Wound tables as Troops.

In **Quick Combat**, roll dice equal to the number of Ships. Basic Ships succeed on a 5–6; Elite Ships succeed on a 4–6.

In **Full Combat**, Basic Ships roll **4d6** and Elite Ships roll **6d6**, just as Basic and Elite Troops do on land.

Bonuses against Light or Heavy Ships apply in the same way as Troop type bonuses. Ships can also carry and land Troops — whether this is possible in a given engagement is at the N.O.'s discretion.

## Ship List

### Galley
**Build Cost:** 250g | **Upkeep:** 250g | **Type:** Light | **Quality:** Basic | **Build Time:** 1 Season
**Bonus:** +1 in Coastal Zones | **Weather:** 5+ | **Navigation:** Coasts, Rivers | **Requirements:** —

A light oared warship, the Galley is effective in the shallow and calm waters of rivers and coastlines. Its low draft allows it to navigate where heavier ships cannot go, making it the preferred patrol vessel for river realms and sheltered bays. It is entirely outmatched in open ocean and fares poorly in rough weather.

### War Galley
**Build Cost:** 500g | **Upkeep:** 500g | **Type:** Heavy | **Quality:** Elite | **Build Time:** 1 Season
**Bonus:** +1 in Coastal Zones | **Weather:** 5+ | **Navigation:** Coasts | **Requirements:** [Cannon Foundry](26-buildings.md#cannon-foundry)

A sturdier version of the Galley, the War Galley is armed with cannons and crewed by hardened sailors. It is formidable in coastal engagements, though like its lighter cousin it cannot venture into the open ocean and needs calm waters to operate.

### Galleass
**Build Cost:** 1,000g | **Upkeep:** 1,000g | **Type:** Heavy | **Quality:** Elite | **Build Time:** 2 Seasons
**Bonus:** +1 in Coastal Zones, +1 vs. Light | **Weather:** 5+ | **Navigation:** Coasts | **Requirements:** [Cannon Foundry](26-buildings.md#cannon-foundry)

A heavy, cumbersome galley armed with a powerful battery of cannons. The Galleass is a siege engine on water — devastating against lighter vessels, but ill-suited for anything beyond calm coastal waters. Its firepower along the shoreline is unmatched.

### Cog
**Build Cost:** 200g | **Upkeep:** 100g | **Type:** Heavy | **Quality:** Basic | **Build Time:** 1 Season
**Bonus:** — | **Weather:** 3+ | **Navigation:** Coasts | **Requirements:** —

A high-sided sailing ship used mainly for trade, the Cog is the workhorse of coastal commerce. While it has little martial value, it can carry significant cargo and withstand moderate seas. Many a Merchant Guild has built its fortune on the humble Cog.

### Holk
**Build Cost:** 1,000g | **Upkeep:** 500g | **Type:** Heavy | **Quality:** Basic | **Build Time:** 2 Seasons
**Bonus:** +1 vs. Light | **Weather:** 3+ | **Navigation:** Coasts | **Requirements:** Powder Mill

An evolution of the Cog, the Holk has been fitted with small cannons to protect its cargo. It is no warship by trade, but it can give a good accounting of itself against lighter vessels in contested waters. A favourite of wealthier merchant captains who must sail where danger lurks.

### Carrack
**Build Cost:** 1,500g | **Upkeep:** 750g | **Type:** Heavy | **Quality:** Elite | **Build Time:** 2 Seasons
**Bonus:** +1 vs. Light | **Weather:** 2+ | **Navigation:** Ocean, Coasts | **Requirements:** [Cannon Foundry](26-buildings.md#cannon-foundry)

> *"She carries enough steel to level a town and enough sail to outrun a man's regrets."* — Unknown Cyrian sailor

An ocean-going warship armed with powerful cannons, the Carrack can brave the open sea and hold its own against nearly anything afloat. It is the backbone of any serious naval power and one of the few vessels capable of crossing from one continent to another.

### Galleon
**Build Cost:** 2,000g | **Upkeep:** 1,000g | **Type:** Heavy | **Quality:** Elite | **Build Time:** 3 Seasons
**Bonus:** +2 vs. Light | **Weather:** 2+ | **Navigation:** Ocean, Coasts | **Requirements:** [Dockyard](26-buildings.md#dockyard), [Cannon Foundry](26-buildings.md#cannon-foundry)

The mightiest of naval vessels — a floating fortress bristling with cannon and crewed by hundreds. Only a Realm with a [Dockyard](26-buildings.md#dockyard) and the [Technical Knowledge](26-buildings.md#gaining-technical-knowledge) to wield it can hope to construct one. A single Galleon can intimidate an entire fleet of lesser ships, and its presence on the horizon has been known to end wars before a shot is fired.

### Caravel
**Build Cost:** 1,500g | **Upkeep:** 750g | **Type:** Light | **Quality:** Basic | **Build Time:** 2 Seasons
**Bonus:** +1 in Open Seas | **Weather:** 3+ | **Navigation:** Ocean, Coasts, Rivers | **Requirements:** [Cannon Foundry](26-buildings.md#cannon-foundry)

> *"Small enough to thread a needle, fast enough to outrun a storm."* — Attributed to several naval captains

A light ocean-going ship armed with light cannons, the Caravel is fast and versatile. It can navigate rivers, coasts, and the open ocean alike — making it the most adaptable vessel afloat. Its light armament means it struggles in a straight fight against heavier warships, but in open water its speed and range give it an edge that raw firepower cannot match.
