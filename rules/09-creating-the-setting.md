# Creating the Setting

Now that we have a world, a map, resources and we know where our players and NPCs are, it is time to create a setting. The setting determines the overall narrative of our game, and provides much needed context to the world our players will be interacting with.

## Creating a Shared History

Discuss ideas with your players. Find out what their concepts are, and start to create a shared history. Do people have similar religions or races? If so, why are they not part of the same Realm? Was one Realm founded by escaped slaves or criminals from another? How were the borders shaped? Who is the oldest Realm, and who is the youngest?

## The Narrative Context

A good setting should have an implied storyline from its context. What are the goals of the players and NPCs? Why are they here? What is at stake?

From our playtests, we found that this question was the key component to creating a good setting: *"Why do I need to change the Status Quo?"* If your players do not feel a need to shape the world around them, then they will simply not interact with it in any meaningful way. Force your players to take sides, act quickly or ally together. While they will certainly have their objectives, give the scenario something the players must react to.

Here are some example concepts you can use for your game, or to give you inspiration.

## Setting Suggestions

### The Fallen Empire

Once all of the Rulers were united, either by common interest, or perhaps just by force, but now the Emperor has died — possibly even assassinated. Three factions have emerged who want to change the order of the world. One faction thinks that the old system was flawed, but that it can be improved. They want to elect a new Emperor from one of the existing Rulers. Another faction wants to seize the opportunity to dissolve the Empire, and grant independence to all the Rulers. While a third remains loyal to the old Empire, and is trying to find a suitable heir to the throne. Perhaps the Emperor had no heir, or maybe his heir is only an infant child.

Give each faction a name, and an NPC leading it. To further split opinion, make sure each faction has different resources at its disposal.

*"Why do I need to change the Status Quo?"* Because civil war threatens the security of my Realm, and if I do not have a say in what the new order will be, it may not be to my liking.

**Inspiration — Japan:** In 1598 Toyotomi Hideyoshi died, leaving his infant son, Hideyori, to inherit his rule. A council of regents were supposed to govern Japan until the boy was old enough, but the Daimyo lords seized the opportunity to take power for themselves. After the decisive battle of Sekigahara in 1600, Tokugawa Ieyasu secured his position as the Shogun of Japan, and his family would rule until the Meiji Restoration in 1868, which finally dissolved the feudal system.

*Is independence best, or are there advantages to an Empire? Who will be the new Emperor — a player, an NPC, or a puppet?*

### A Brave New World

All of our players are small lords of a much larger kingdom or empire. They have come to colonise a new world, supposedly rich in resources. However, they have not found it entirely uninhabited. When creating your regions, consider having twice as many neutral ones. Keep in mind though that the locals won't recognise these borders in the same way as these foreign visitors do. Alternatively, instead of additional neutral territories, have a large disparate Empire take up most of the map.

*"Why do I need to change the Status Quo?"* Because everyone is going to expand rapidly in this new world, and I will be pushed away from the best spots if I don't act quickly.

**Inspiration — Colonial America:** As early as the sixteenth century, Europeans were in Northern America. Beaver pelts were a much sought after resource at the time for hat-making.[^13] As trade grew, so did the trading posts from which the merchants conducted their business. Soon these grew into towns and eventually cities. By the mid-eighteenth century, France and Britain controlled large parts of what is now the United States and Canada. In 1754 the Seven Years War (French-Indian War) began, which pitted Britain and France's colonial interests against each other. The Native American Iroquois and Huron Confederacies were also active participants, and played the French and British against each other for their own political interests.

**Inspiration — Mughal India:** At the same time, the British and French were also fighting for dominance in India. Here their colonies were not as large or as widespread because India was already densely populated. However, the old Moghul Empire was collapsing, and both the British and French played local Nawab lords against each other. Support from foreign powers could change the tide of battle. By the end of the Seven Years War, Britain had pushed France out of India, and much of North America. Now the local populations had no political conflict to use to their advantage. A new status quo had been created, with the fledgling British Empire free to solidify its rule.[^14]

*How developed is the new world? How many factions are there? What new resources, or technologies, do they have? How do I keep these away from enemies, and profit from them myself?*

### Impending Doom

Something...is coming. An apocalyptic event awaits our players and they must prepare for it. The Rulers of the world will have to put their differences aside and stand as one, or else face oblivion. Perhaps the Orcish raiders threaten to pillage every city, or the Mongol Hordes want to expand their empire by blood and fire, or maybe the great Dragon-Lich is amassing his undead forces and is preparing to break free from his prison crypt, and will subjugate all the living beings of this world to his unholy will!

Explain to your players that they have a certain number of turns to prepare for the threat. You don't necessarily have to tell them what it is, but we do recommend leaving clues for them to piece together what it is they will need to defend themselves.

*"Why do I need to change the Status Quo?"* Because we're all going to be cut into pieces, have our souls bound to their evil whims, and have our heads impaled on stakes... And if we're very very lucky, they'll do it in that order!

**Inspiration — Thermopylae:** In Ancient Greece, there was no such thing as one united body. Instead there were lots of small city-states. The most powerful of which was Athens, and its biggest rival was the city of Sparta. However, when the Persian Empire decided to expand onto Grecian land, people did recognise the need to ally together against this larger threat. At the Battle of Thermopylae, an alliance of Spartans, Athens and other city-states tried to hold back the Persian forces. They were defeated, but it bought the Athenians some time. Athens still bore the brunt of the war, and it was still the Athenian navy who finally sent the Persians packing,[^15] but for a brief period these rival cities banded together for the greater good of all.

*How long do we have? Where will our enemy come from? What do I need in order to protect my Realm from oblivion?*
