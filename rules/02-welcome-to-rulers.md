# Welcome to Rulers

Rulers is a tabletop roleplaying game of conquest and politics, where players will craft their own civilisation and forge their own dynasty. The game is played with 4–6 Players and 1 Narrative Overlord, whose job it is to "referee" the game. To take actions in the game, Players will send Reports to the Narrative Overlord by email, letter, private courier or carrier pigeon (as you prefer).

This book — the *Rulers: Core Rulebook* — details the Rulers System as well as the instructions on how to play the Rulers roleplaying game. Tabletop Roleplaying games are a wonderful social activity of group storytelling, and Rulers is no different. As such, the game all happens in your collective imagination. Feel free to use maps, diagrams and even figurines if you want to, but do not feel obliged to do so. We'll get into all the detailed rules of building up an Empire, Recruiting [Armies](27-armies.md), and Converting the masses. But first, let's look at some of the basic concepts.

## The Narrative Overlord

A game of Rulers has 3–6 Players and one Narrative Overlord (N.O.). It is the task of the N.O. to run the game and fundamentally captain the game as a sort of referee for the story. It is up to the Narrative Overlord to make sure all Player actions remain reasonable, that there are consequences for their actions, and to aid the narrative development of the game. While at the same time, an N.O. should make sure that everyone is having fun. From time to time, this may mean saying N.O. to their players![^1]

The N.O. also controls all Rulers, factions and organisations not directly controlled by the Players themselves. This includes all [Nobles](16-nobles.md), [Guilds, Orders and Societies](18-guilds-orders-and-societies.md) in a Player's Realm, who each have their own political agenda. For this reason, you can have a second N.O. if help is needed to control all these political entities. Alternatively, hand over control of certain NPCs to Players. Just make sure that they act according to that character, and not the interests of the Player.

The N.O. goes first in a game of Rulers. [Political Actions](06-actions-in-rulers.md) are not limited for entities the N.O. controls, but they must still be able to pay for any [Buildings](26-buildings.md), [Troops](27-armies.md) or other actions they might perform.

## The Players

Players in a game of Rulers control the [Ruler](12-creating-your-ruler.md) of a state. This is their in-game avatar. Maybe your family line has always ruled, perhaps you were elected to your current position, or you were quite simply ordained to rule by [Insert Deity/Pantheon of choice here]. Whatever the reason, you Rule!

Each Player controls the Ruler of their Realm, and can roleplay actions as the Rulers they control. As Ruler you govern your Realm, the [Territory](08-territories-and-resources.md) you control. You can levy [Taxes](23-making-money.md) to raise funds which can be used to Construct [Buildings](26-buildings.md) and Recruit [Troops](27-armies.md). In addition to this, a Ruler can perform [Political Actions](06-actions-in-rulers.md).

Your responsibilities are to protect your Realm and the property of your people. It is not your duty to meddle in their everyday lives.[^2] The economy of your Realm will largely take care of itself and does not need a Ruler's micro-management. The people of your Realm, from Farmer to Nobleman, will be unhappy if the Realm runs out of [Food](20-food-for-the-people.md), or if their livelihood is threatened, but these responsibilities are not enshrined in law. They will simply take matters into their own hands if there is too much [Turmoil](21-turmoil.md).

[^2]: Although not every Ruler got that memo.

## The Objective

The objective of a game of Rulers can vary. It can be discussed by all the Players and the N.O., but the N.O. has the final say. Ultimately, it is the N.O.'s job to decide where the story goes. A game of Rulers can be played until an invading army has been defeated, the secrets of an ancient civilisation discovered, or as long as you are having fun playing.

Whatever the goal may be, it is a good idea for a Ruler to have their own goal in mind, and to roleplay their Ruler's individual ambitions.
