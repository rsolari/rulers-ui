# Food for the People

Food for the People A society can only function if its subjects are
well fed. Hungry subjects are restless subjects, and if not for the
welfare of the Realm, it is in the interest of the Ruler's own
wellbeing, to keep the granaries stocked. Food is produced by
Settlements. Typically, this is done by various agrarian communities
that orbit them. A town for instance, might have a few farmsteads dotted
in the foothills around it. The Food produced by these Settlements can
be a mix of several Foods that makes sense for the Realm to have,
providing that they remain as basic Foods that could not be mistaken for
Luxury Industries. Sake and Rice, Cheese and Milk, Wine and Beef, Vodka
and Potatoes would all be suitable examples of Food and drink produced.
However, Honey and Ginger Ale or Cinnamon Buns and Coffee are not the
foundations of a balanced diet for a whole society.29 The numbers
involved in Food production and need do not only reflect the quantity of
Food being produced, but also the variety and the healthiness of the
diet of the Realms denizens. Food Production For every empty Building

If there is not enough Food to support all the Settle - ments in the
Realm, then Turmoil will quickly increase, and eventually Starvation
will take its toll. Sizeable military Garrisons will also require Food
in order to keep Troops stationed there. This includes free stand - ing
Forts and Castles that are not a part of a Settlement. Territory Limits
While certain Buildings and Special Actions can increase the amount of
Food produced in the Realm, there comes a point when a Territory can
longer support further culti - vation. One Territory cannot produce more
than 30 Food, before bonuses from Buildings or Special Actions. While
bonuses from Buildings or Special Actions can push one Territory to
produce more than 30 Food, in order to exceed this, a Realm will have to
expand its bor - ders into new Territories. Food Shortages Food can
become sparse before this begins to cause seri - ous resentment, or even
starvation. If there is not enough Food in the Realm to meet de - mand
for one Season, this upsets the populace. From a narrative perspective
would make them more likely to act against the Ruler, but has no
immediate effect from a game mechanics point of view. If there still is
a Food shortage for another Season, Tur - moil in the Realm increases by
1/4 of its current value, rounding up. This is raises to 1/2 for each
successive Season of Food Shortage. Turmoil added in this way does not
disappear until there is enough Food for everyone in the Realm for 2
Seasons.
