Armies Recruiting Troops Whether a Ruler wishes to see their banner
flying from every tower in land, or simply to protect the livelihood of
their people, a Realm needs Troops. As every subject of the Realm owes
allegiance to their Lord, and the Lords in turn, owe service to their
Ruler, calling Troops to action is relatively simple affair.32 However,
someone has to toil the land and make the wares people use in their
daily lives. Depending on the size of the Realm's Settlements, and their
number, will determine how many Troops a Realm can support. Village = 6
Troops each Town = 10 Troops each City = 20 Troops each However, it
takes time to mobilise Troops and inspect their equipment. Each
Settlement also determines how many Troops may be Recruited each Season.
Village = 4 Troop per Season Town = 6 Troops per Season City = 8 Troops
per Season Basic Troops might be fortunate enough to receive some
training, but even with their simple equipment, they can prove very
effective in large numbers. Elite Troops come from the Nobility of your
Realm. They have much more time to not just practice, but to even master
their martial training. In addition, they can afford to procure much
more expensive equipment. Once recruited, Troops immediately report to
the Garri - son they were assigned. This can be Settlement or a For
tification. They are commanded by the governing Noble of this
Settlement, or must be assigned to the command of Noble. If a collection
of Troops - an Army - wants to travel to a location that does not have a
Garrison, they must be led by a Noble. Nobles who fight well in your
service, and lead you to Victory, will expect an increase in their
holdings and rank. It costs nothing to Recruit Troops, but it does cost
to keep them fed, train them once recruited, and maintain their
equipment. This is all done by paying Upkeep. In order to recruit a type
of Troop, the Realm needs access to the necessary equipment. These
